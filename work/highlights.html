Work highlights

Customer-Solution Fit (CSF)

When I joined Fyle as a senior designer, we were focused on the Indian market, but during COVID, Fyle's business was severely impacted. This forced a strategic pivot to the U.S. market, changing the customer base entirely.

Operating in a new market, managing number of custimer interviews and demo requests was exciting but overwhelming at a business level. At a team level, it was even more intense—I was stepping into management for the first time, navigating new responsibilities while driving critical decisions, faster execution and sometimes making my hands dirty too.

Strategic decisions:

We focused more on improving the core workflow as part of the strategy to cater to US Customers' specific needs.

Core workflow refers to the entire process—from submitting expenses by the spender to closing them by the admin and updating the accountant book like QBO, QBD etc.

Followings are some of the key initiatives that I led as per our strategic themes. I was fortunate enough to work with a team of talented designers.

↳ Increasing Camera Capture feature from 40.2% to 72.7% (~81% increase in usage)

We undertook the mobile app revamp to create a strong first impression (front door effect) and address issues like consistency, scalability, navigation, confusing UX copy, and feedback. These improvements helped us deliver great partnership demos in the global market.

Out of many, We focused on increasing Camera capture feature usage as it helps user capturing expense information in the most intuitve way with AI capabilities within seconds. Initially, this feature was called "InstaFyle," but customers found it unclear. Only 40% of users adopted it, while 51% still preferred manual expense entry, which often took over 42 seconds per expense.

↳ Reducing Accounting Integration Set up timing from 5-7 days to 15 min

The accounting integration platform serves as a bridge between Fyle and a business's accounting books, enabling the seamless import and export of expense-related information.

Fyle's initial accounting integration was an MVP built by engineers. However, as the business grew, experience-related challenges emerged, leading to inefficiencies, increased dependency on the customer success team, and scalability issues. View case study

Hence we relooked at all touchpoints to simplify it. The new experience attracted many accounting firms to partner with Fyle later. These accounting partners, in turn, brought in numerous customers, helping Fyle surpass $1M in revenue.

Key Outcomes
  • The adaptation rate for the new design reached 95% within a quarter.
  • The time taken for setting up integration was reduced from 5 -7 days to 15min.
  • Customers took less than 20 seconds to resolve mapping errors.

↳ Helping customers integrate their own existing card & getting featured in Forbes

Businesses provide corporate cards to employees for managing expenses, but Fyle’s existing infrastructure required a wait time of 1 to 30 days for transactions to reflect in the system, delaying reconciliation for the entire organization.

Instead of issuing new cards like others, Fyle leveraged the existing card ecosystem. Our partnership with Visa and Mastercard enabled users to integrate their own cards, instantly capturing receipts via SMS the moment a transaction occurred, thus reduing the TTV to seconds. This led to 85% of user adoptions who sent receipts via SMS.

The design particulary helped users integrating cards in a more efficient way on top of an existing eco system of adding cards via Yodlee. Visa/Mastercard team approved the design in one shot.

This groundbreaking feature disrupted the expense management industry, unlocking access to the massive $1.5 trillion card spend market and earning Fyle a feature in Forbes.

Product Market Fit (PMF)

With a steady inflow of demos and improved core offerings, it was clear that Fyle had achieved PMF in the U.S. SMB space..

Next, we focused on driving revenue growth while keeping churn within 11-15%.

Key priorities:

  • Boosting NPS from 35 to 50+ by collecting user feedback and driving actionable improvements.
  • Reworking core features that caused drop-offs.
  • Introducing automation in the core workflow to enhance efficiency.
